Plan Your Day

1. Sugar Loaf Mountain

Sugarloaf Mountain is a small picturesque treasure which is an effective hike after a few minutes of the city. This path is very short and will take one to a rocky peak where one can view the ocean and other islands. It is ideal as a speedy adventure, sunset sightseeing or as a relaxing break into the nature.

2. Colliery Dam Park

One of the best places to have a quiet walk in Nanaimo is Colliery Dam Park, where a person is free to enjoy a walk in nature. Curving paths lead around reflectsive waters of the dam, to form a serene atmosphere worth relaxing and enjoying the view. The tall trees, the slow singing of birds and shadowy walks make each step taken seem relaxing and peaceful. Regardless of whether you walk, jog or are just enjoying the scenery, it is an ideal place to breathe slow and renew you.

3. Neck point

“Where the sky softens, the waves whisper, and time slows down.”
In Neck Point Park, everything is slowed down in the most desirable manner by nature. Several coastal paths are woven through the rocky coast and silent woods, and lead to the open sea, which seems nearly interminable. It is a relaxing energy to the park to watch the sun set on the horizon, to hear the waves play on the rocks, or stand motionless and absorb it all in. It is a type of destination where you do not require an agenda, only a time to relax and feel one of the most serene coastal retreats on Nanaimo.

4. Little Mountain

Little Mountain is a hidden gem that offers some of the most breathtaking views in the city. A short and easy hike leads you to a stunning viewpoint overlooking Departure Bay and the coastal islands. It’s the perfect spot to catch a golden sunset or enjoy a quiet moment above the city. Surrounded by forest and fresh ocean air, the climb feels refreshing and rewarding. Whether you’re chasing views or simply a peaceful escape, Little Mountain is a must-visit in Nanaimo.

5.Little Qualicum Falls

The rushing waterfalls and lush forest scenery at Little Qualicum Falls Provincial Park are a sight to behold. You'll be rewarded with powerful cascades that tumble through rocky canyons if you follow the easy trails. An unforgettable experience is created by the roaring water and cool mist in the air. Every step feels like a picture-perfect moment thanks to wooden bridges and picturesque viewpoints. A trip to Qualicum Falls is a must if you want to see nature at its most beautiful and raw.

6. Cameron Lake

Qualicum Falls is a must if you want to see nature at its most beautiful and raw. Cameron Lake is a stunning getaway on the side of the road where towering mountain views meet crystal-clear water. Just a short drive from Nanaimo, it’s the perfect spot to pause, unwind, and take in the scenery. On calm days, the lake reflects the surrounding forest like a mirror—pure postcard perfection. Whether you’re stopping for a quick photo, a peaceful picnic, or a refreshing dip, it never disappoints. If you’re chasing that serene, hidden-lake feeling, Cameron Lake is a must-stop along the way.

7. Gabriola Island

Nanaimo is just a short ferry ride away from the charming island of Gabriola. Known as the “Isle of the Arts,” it’s filled with creative energy, coastal beauty, and laid-back vibes. From stunning sandstone formations at Malaspina Galleries to quiet beaches and forest trails, there’s so much to explore. The tranquility of the island makes it ideal for a day trip or scenic getaway. Gabriola Island is a must-see if you want to unwind, explore, and take in the charm of the west coast.

8. Cox Bay Beach

From Nanaimo, the scenic drive to Cox Bay Beach, a breathtaking west coast destination, is well worth it. It is a popular destination for surfers and sunset chasers alike due to its expansive sandy shores and powerful waves. The entire beach feels alive with energy as the waves come in and the sky turns golden. The views are unforgettable, whether you are walking along the shoreline or watching surfers maneuver through the waves. Cox Bay Beach is a must-visit adventure for that authentic wild Pacific experience.
